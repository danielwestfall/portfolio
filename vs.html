VS gaming app

ux/ui design • by daniel

The product:

Versus is an app that records gaming achievements and lets gamers build communities and send challenges to one another.

The problem:

A lack of engagement with users when there is a mostly static list of leaderboards.

Target audience:

As the pandemic hit and lockdowns started, gaming was a huge way to spend time and socalize with friends users couldn't see in person. Online communities needed new ways to interact with each other and to share their gaming experiences. VS Gaming was born, a way to showcase gaming achivements and challenge your friends.

The goal:

To increase user engagement and traffic with the app by creating an interactive community of players.

Key Challenges and Constraints:

Some of our key challenges and constraints were creating a easy to navigate user flow that let people find a game, send a customized challenge, and check on the status of their sent and received challenges.

Low Fidelity design

After creating paper wireframes using methods such as crazy eights and pulling the best of each iteration we started moving our designs to the digital space. We created a low fidelity wireframe of the app and created a mockup of the app.

Low Fidelity prototyping

Here is the VS Lo-fi Prototype created with Figma.

We used the wireframes and created a user flow that could simulate the essential portions of the app. Since this project was adding a new user flow to an app, we went through multiple iterations and smoothed out the challenge user flow.

Usability study : findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.
  • Round 1:
    • Users want to easily access challenges
    • Users wanted more diversity in achievements and games
    • Account page needed redesign
  • Round 2:
    • A page was needed just to show challenges
    • Confirmation pages were needed for submitted options

Mobile first design

We developed high fidelity wireframes and prototypes for the app that scale for mobile devices through the user flow.

High Fidelity design and prototyping

Here is the VS Hi-fi Prototype created with Figma.

Here are the mockups turned into a high-fidelity prototype. This the mobile version set for a 360 x 800 screen. The challenge flow is represented, including the home page, menu, profile page, game list page, game details page, and the challenges and results pages.

My role:

Lead UX designer, UX researcher, full stack developer.

Responsibilities:

User research, wireframing, prototyping, front-end developing, back-end developing.

Impact

The app makes users feel like they’re in a community and not just looking at a scoreboard.

What I learned:

This process has helped me realized that the smallest considerations in design can change the accessibility for so many users. Usability studies and peer feedback helped guide the changes and improvements in design.

Next Steps

  • Continue the usability study iteration on the design of the app to make sure all pain points are addressed.
  • Conduct more user research to determine any additional areas to strengthen the feel of community.
