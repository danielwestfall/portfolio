PhaseThru

ux/ui design • by daniel

The product:

PhaseThru is an app that allows hands free/voice control of YouTube DIY videos with a database of steps to each video for easy “phasing”.

The problem:

Trying to complete some DIY activities following YouTube how-to videos, but having to repeatedly stop and restart parts of the videos. Some activities can keep the user from being able to touch a device to restart the video.

Target audience:

As the pandemic hit videos for “beginners” got over 9 million views in 2020 alone. When following tutorials on subjects such as cooking, makeup application, auto repair, and other activities the user often can’t use their hands to scrub through videos. Besides situational challenges, disabilities such as hearing loss can make it hard to follow along in time with videos and closed captions move as just fast as the video and crucial information often goes by rather quickly. Often video descriptions are hidden by default in videos as well.

The goal:

Create a voice controlled interface that allows you to “phase thru” a video as you need, an interface to define the phases, and a database to store them for use by any user.

Key Challenges and Constraints:

Some of our key challenges and constraints were creating a easy to use interface that could easily be followed along with and legible while the user is unable to interact with the device.

Usability study : findings

Our usability studies helped us determine pain points in the designs and helped us refine our prototypes.
  • Higher visibility in phase descriptions
  • More efficient user flow for phased videos
  • Users wanted to preview and edit phases found in the database

Mobile first design

We developed high fidelity wireframes and prototypes for the app that scale from mobile to desktop using progressive enhancement techniques.

My role:

Lead UX designer, UX researcher, full stack developer.

Responsibilities:

User research, wireframing, prototyping, front-end developing, back-end developing.

Impact

PhaseThru hopes to be the essential way for DIY videos to be utilized. By increasing DIY successes and making happier users the number of views and likes of a video could increase, encouraging video creators to make more content.

What I learned:

I learned how going through the entire UX design process gives clarity to the design that you can’t really get from just jumping into the code. It also makes the development process easier to start when all the elements are already laid out.

Next Steps

  • Conduct research on how successful the app is by measuring user and creator feedback.
  • Add more ways of navigating phased videos, such as hand gestures or device tilting.
  • Provide ways users can give feedback about sets of phases in the database and share their DIY results.
