digital cents

ux/ui design • by daniel

The product:

Digital cents is a cryptocurrency exchange for people new to trading. The goal of digital cents is to be a bridge for people used to online banking to investing in cryptocurrency.

The problem:

Cryptocurrency exchanges can be very complicated and can be nerve-racking for new investors.

Target audience:

Digital cents is a new and emerging cryptocurrency exchange. It is a bridge between people used to online banking and people who are new to investing in cryptocurrency.

The goal:

Design a new user friendly trading flow that is simple to understand and easy to use.

Key Challenges and Constraints:

Our key challenge was to design a user friendly trading flow that is simple to understand and easy to use.

Low Fidelity design

We used the wireframes and created a user flow that could simulate the essential portions of the app. Since this project was adding a new user flow to an existing app, we went through multiple iterations and smoothed out the challenge user flow.

Usability study : findings

These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study:
  • Users find it easier to think in terms of their local currency
  • Users get frustrated going back and forth and adjust amounts and currencies
  • Users are most comfortable when things are laid out simply
  • Users want to know when transactions will be final

Web first design

We developed high fidelity wireframes and prototypes for the app that scale from desktop to mobile using graceful degradation techniques.

My role:

Lead UX designer, UX researcher, full stack developer.

Responsibilities:

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, and responsive design.

Impact

Our target users shared that the design made it much simpler to create a trade between currencies, and that it was a less stressful experience for new traders.

What I learned:

I learned that you can always learn from experiencing something and that a good design can explain a process and be a comfort for users in a new experience.

Next Steps

  • Conduct follow-up usability testing on the new versions of the app
  • Identify any additional areas of need
  • Ideate on new features to help more users learn about the processes in a crypto exchange
